Dave, 42. Bald. Flannel. Engineering manager who'd rather be on his boat. The same Levi's he's owned for a decade because why fix what isn't broken? Sunday is the Packers game from his worn leather recliner, phone in hand, half-watching, mostly scrolling. Saturday is his daughter's soccer game and maybe Home Depot. His wife manages the family calendar. He shows up where he's told. The NFL is background noise, not his identity. No disruption. That's all he wants. His version of being an involved dad is showing up to the soccer game on Saturday. Not signing up for programs.
